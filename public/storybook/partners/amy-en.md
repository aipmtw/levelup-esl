# Partner — Amy (new-parent / 0–3 specialist channel) — **master partner**

> Amy is one of Mark's **three direct master partners**, alongside Audrey and Arita. She can recruit and coach sub-partners under her umbrella via the master-partner model. See [`../concept/master-partner-model-en.md`](../concept/master-partner-model-en.md) and [`family-governance-en.md`](family-governance-en.md).

## Who she is
Amy is **Mark's daughter** and has a **1-year-old son** (Mark's grandson). She is already a **paying customer of physical personalized kid books** — she knows the category firsthand, knows the price points that feel worth it, and knows what makes a parent say "yes" or "no" at the checkout.

She is the single most qualified voice on the 0–3 tier on the whole platform.

## Target
- **Primary:** her own son, and the babies/toddlers in her **new-parent social circle** (first-time moms, baby-shower friends, parents-of-1-year-olds groups, preschool-entry families).
- **Age specialty:** **0–3 tier** — the tier where Audrey (older kids) and Arita (school-age English students) have less focus. Amy fills the coverage gap.

## Go-to-market channel
- **Peer-to-peer within her new-parent circle.** Baby shower gifts, first-birthday keepsakes, bilingual baby books for mixed-language households.
- **Demonstration by use:** Amy literally uses the product on her own son, every day. The book is in front of the grandson's eyes in the stroller, in the car, at bedtime. Her friends see it working.
- **Physical-book-adjacent positioning.** Amy already buys physical books — her pitch isn't "replace physical books with an app", it's **"a personalized book that also has narration and can be re-read in airplane mode"**. Less threatening, more complementary.

## Languages
- **Traditional Chinese (zh-TW / zh-Hant)** — baseline. Traditional only, never Simplified. Critical at 0–3 where parents read aloud and pronunciation matters.
- **English (en)** — optional. Many new parents in Amy's circle want early English exposure.
- **Japanese (ja-JP)** — optional. Strong fit if Amy's circle has Japanese-exposure families.

## Unique product angle — "first books" for first-time parents
Amy's niche is **the first 5 books of a child's life**. Things like:
- "{child_name} 認識自己" (I know myself) — first words for body parts, family members.
- "{child_name} 的一天" (My day) — morning routine, meals, bath, bedtime.
- "{child_name} 的家" (My home) — familiar objects around the house.
- "{child_name} 的第一生日" (My first birthday) — keepsake book for the big day.
- "{child_name} 喜歡的動物" (Animals I love) — early vocabulary, soft illustrations.

Very simple text, very large illustrations, heavy use of the child's real name and real photos (optional), narration as the core feature (parents don't always have voice to read aloud at 2am).

## Amy's grandson as the first real end-user
Mark's grandson is, by coincidence of who Amy is, the **first real end user** of the entire product. Every book Amy generates for her son is:

1. **Real usage, not a synthetic demo.** The grandson doesn't know the difference between "demo" and "real" — he just knows whether he wants to hear the book again.
2. **Raw unfiltered feedback.** Amy → Mark will report every bug, every slow page, every weird pronunciation, every image that confused her son. This is product intelligence Mark cannot buy.
3. **Natural showcase.** Other parents in Amy's circle see her son engaging with the PWA in airplane mode on a plane, in a car. That's live demo in context, not a landing page.
4. **Mark's proudest ship event.** The first time Mark's grandson points at the phone and says "阿公 (grandpa), 再一次" — that's the product's real launch.

**Privacy:** the grandson's books live in Amy's private workspace. Mark's global admin override must **not** be used to browse Amy's workspace casually (see [`family-governance-en.md`](family-governance-en.md)).

## Sub-partner recruiting
Amy can recruit sub-partners from her new-parent circle — friends who want to offer personalized books to their own baby-group friends. This is especially natural because new-parent circles are small, tight, and built on trust. Same master-partner model as Audrey and Arita.

## Starting point
No existing `markluce.ai/amy` page yet — Amy is onboarding fresh. Her first action is to generate a real book for her own son, use it for a week with him, and file a tracked list of fixes/feature requests. That becomes both her demo work and the first shaped-by-real-use feedback for the 0–3 tier templates.

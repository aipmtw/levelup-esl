# Target audience

## Primary target (locked)
**Children age 0–12.** This is the only target market for markluce.ai.

**Language mix:**
- **Traditional Chinese (zh-TW / zh-Hant)** — baseline, always. Never Simplified.
- **English** — required add-on.
- **Japanese** — optional add-on.

## Two layers

### Mark's customers (who Mark sells to)
**Partners** — small operators who reach parents of 0–12 kids. Different channels:

- **Audrey channel** — working parents with their own kids + parent social circles. Peer-to-peer community.
- **Arita channel** — kid English teachers with existing students. 1:1 teacher-parent consultation.
- **Future partners** — same pattern: any operator with a legitimate channel into 0–12 parents (daycare operators, pediatric clinics, children's librarians, kids' illustrators, etc.).

### Partners' customers (end users — Mark does NOT bill these)
**Parents of 0–12 kids in Traditional-Chinese-speaking markets.**
- Traditional Chinese native.
- Want English exposure for their kids (strong in Taiwan / HK / overseas diaspora).
- Some want Japanese as a second foreign language.
- Value personalized, re-readable, offline-capable keepsakes.
- Willing to pay a trusted partner (their teacher, their parent-friend) — not a random SaaS.

## Geography
- **Primary:** Taiwan (zh-TW Traditional + LINE native).
- **Secondary:** Hong Kong, overseas Traditional-Chinese diaspora (US, Canada, Australia).
- **Tertiary:** Japan — via the ja-JP optional language. Future sub-brand possibility (see [`../concept/future-domain-en.md`](../concept/future-domain-en.md)).

## Why LINE-first
- Target parents live on LINE (TW / JP native).
- LINE Login already working.
- Partners run their own LINE groups — Mark doesn't need to build a social layer.
- PWA can be LINE-account-gated, matching how parents already think about access.

## Out of primary target
- Adult English learners (Arita's legacy surface) — kept live but not M1 priority.
- Markets without LINE penetration (Mainland China, Europe, most of Americas) — not addressed at M1.
- Simplified Chinese markets — explicitly excluded by the Traditional-only rule.
